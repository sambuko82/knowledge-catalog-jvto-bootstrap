# Overview

Tumpak Sewu is a wide, multi-tiered "curtain" waterfall (roughly 120 m high)
in Lumajang Regency, East Java, sometimes called "Java's Niagara" for the
many streams falling across a curved cliff into a canyon.

# Visiting

- A rim viewpoint is reached by a short, easy walk from parking.
- An optional descent into the canyon is steep (about 300 steps, with ropes
  in places) over wet, slippery terrain; guides assess fitness and brief the
  route before starting, and the decision to descend stays with the guest.
- Helmets for the canyon section are provided by the local site management,
  not by JVTO.
