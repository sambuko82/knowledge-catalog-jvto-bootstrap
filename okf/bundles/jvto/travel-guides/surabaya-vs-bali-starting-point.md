# Overview

JVTO runs private packages from both Surabaya and Bali. The destinations are
the same East Java volcanoes and waterfalls; what changes is the logistics —
where you begin, the direction of travel, and the first transfer time. There
are more routes from Surabaya, while Bali-origin routes add a ferry crossing.
Choose the origin that fits your flights and timeline.

# The Bali Ferry Crossing

Bali-origin routes cross the Ketapang–Gilimanuk strait. Actual sailing time
is roughly 45–60 minutes; itineraries that look like a 2-hour crossing are
usually counting the 1-hour clock change between Bali (WITA) and Java
(WIB). The ferry runs around the clock, roughly hourly, with a full stop
during the Balinese Nyepi day each year.

Ferry tickets are online-only via the official Ferizy platform — on-site
purchase has been abolished, and a GPS geofence blocks online purchase near
the ports, so tickets must be held before arriving at the harbor. JVTO
pre-books the crossing on Bali-origin tours. For reference, the regular
adult passenger fare is IDR 11,100 under tariff decree KM 131/2024 (in
force since November 2024); vehicle classes and holiday periods carry
higher tariffs, and fares change by decree — figures are as of the
2026-07-07 source check.
