# Travel Guides

## Concepts

* [Best Time to Visit East Java](best-time-to-visit.md) - When to go — the dry season gives the clearest conditions for East Java's volcanoes and waterfalls; the wet season brings cloud and muddier access.
* [Mount Bromo vs Kawah Ijen](bromo-vs-ijen.md) - Which volcano suits you — Bromo is an easier sunrise caldera experience, Ijen is a more demanding pre-dawn hike for the blue fire and acidic crater lake.
* [How Many Days Do You Need?](how-many-days.md) - Matching trip length to what you want to see — from a single Bromo sunrise to a multi-day circuit adding Ijen, waterfalls, and the coast.
* [Packing & Fitness](packing-and-fitness.md) - What to bring and the fitness each destination needs — warm layers for cold pre-dawn starts, grippy footwear for volcanic and canyon terrain, and waterproofing where you get wet.
* [Safety on Tours](safety-on-tours.md) - How JVTO makes safety decisions — a written framework with pre-hike briefings, trained guides, proper equipment, and contingency procedures.
* [Starting from Surabaya vs Bali](surabaya-vs-bali-starting-point.md) - Choosing your origin — the destinations are the same volcanoes and waterfalls; the difference is logistics, transfer times, and (for Bali) a ferry crossing.
* [Weather, Volcanic Activity & Closures](weather-and-closures.md) - What happens when conditions change — Bromo and Ijen are active volcanoes, and JVTO follows authority instructions and activates a Plan-B if access is restricted.
